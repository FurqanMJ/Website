Charminar

The Charminar, an iconic monument in Hyderabad, was built in **1591** by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the **end of a deadly plague** that devastated the city. What makes it unique is its hidden **secret tunnel** rumored to connect it to the Golconda Fort, nearly 10 kilometers away. This underground passage was believed to be an escape route for the royal family during times of siege. Although the tunnel remains undiscovered, it adds a layer of mystery to the architectural marvel!
